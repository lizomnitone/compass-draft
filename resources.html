Frequently Asked Questions

▼

Do you do small jobs?

Yes. We are doing small jobs all the time, some of them for tiny parts and small quantities.

Do you only do work for businesses?

No. We work for individuals for personal projects as well as what we do for businesses.

Can I supply my own material?

Yes. We can supply material but will work with yours if that's your preference. If you like, we can quote material and labour separately so you can decide if you'd want us to supply material or not.

Do you only work with steel?

No. We work with almost all metals, including carbon steels, stainless steels, aluminum, copper, brass, spring steel, and many others.

Do you do any fabrication or only bending?

We do some fabrication, mostly in steel, but we can also weld aluminum. We also offer stud-welding for concrete anchors and some machining services in our in-house machine shop. These extra capabilities make it easier for our customers to do one-stop shopping.

Glossary

▼

A

Angle Correction

Adjustment made to the initial bend angle to account for springback, ensuring the final bend meets specification.

Annealing

Heat treatment process that softens metal and relieves internal stress, making material more ductile and easier to bend.

B

Back-Bending

A secondary bending operation performed in the opposite direction to correct or refine a previous bend.

Bend Allowance

The amount of material consumed or stretched during the bending process, used in calculations for flat pattern layouts.

Bend Radius

The measurement of the curve created by a bend, typically measured from the center of the curve to the inside edge of the bent material.

C

Centerline Radius (CLR)

The radius measured from the center of the curve to the centerline of the material (for tubes/pipe). The primary specification for bend design.

Coil Bending

A specialized bending technique that creates continuous spiral or helical curves for cooktop burners and custom equipment.

Cold Bending

Bending material at room temperature without heating, commonly used for most commercial bending applications.

D

Die

A shaped tool that forms the outer profile of the bend. Different dies produce different bend radii and shapes.

Draw Bending

A bending method using a mandrel (internal support) and die to create precise, tight-radius bends for pipe and tube work.

Deformation

The change in shape or dimensions of material as a result of bending. Can be elastic (recovers) or plastic (permanent).

E

Elastic Recovery

The tendency of material to partially return to its original shape after bending stress is removed, also known as springback.

Elongation

The amount a material stretches during bending, expressed as a percentage of original length.

F

Fabrication

The process of building, assembling, or constructing metal parts. Includes cutting, bending, welding, and finishing operations.

Flatness Tolerance

The allowable deviation from a perfectly flat surface, specified in engineering drawings.

Flat Pattern

The unfolded, two-dimensional representation of a bent part showing where bends will occur.

G

Gauge (Wire)

A standardized measurement system for wire or sheet metal thickness. Lower gauge numbers indicate thicker material.

Grain Direction

The directional alignment of crystals in metal, which affects bending performance and material strength.

H

Heat Treating

Controlled heating and cooling of metal to modify its properties, hardness, and workability.

Hot Bending

Bending metal at elevated temperatures to reduce stress and allow for tighter bends in materials that would otherwise be difficult to bend cold.

I

Induction Bending

A bending method using electromagnetic induction to heat a localized area of the pipe while bending, reducing springback and enabling tighter radii.

Inside Radius

The measurement of the curve on the inner surface of a bend, from the center point of the curve.

M

Mandrel

An internal support tool inserted into a tube or pipe during bending to prevent collapse, wrinkles, or deformation of the inside surface.

Material Specification

The documented properties and grade of material, including composition, strength, and workability characteristics.

N

Neutral Axis

The imaginary line through the thickness of material where there is neither compression nor elongation during bending.

Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)

Inspection methods that evaluate material properties without damaging the part, including ultrasonic, X-ray, and dye penetrant testing.

O

Offset Bending

Creating a parallel displacement in a pipe or tube by making two bends in opposite directions.

Outside Radius

The measurement of the curve on the outer surface of a bend, measured from the center point of the curve.

P

Plastic Deformation

Permanent change in shape of material that does not recover after stress is removed, the desired result in bending operations.

Pressure Testing

Hydraulic or pneumatic testing of bent pipes and tubes to verify structural integrity and pressure ratings.

Profile

The cross-sectional shape of material being bent, such as round tube, square tube, channel, angle, or flat bar.

R

Radius

The distance from the center of a curve to any point on the curve, expressed in inches or millimeters.

Roller

A rotating cylindrical tool used in roll bending to progressively deform material into a curved shape.

Roll Bending

A bending method using rotating rollers to create smooth, large-radius curves in various profiles and materials.

S

Schedule

A standardized wall thickness classification for pipes and tubes, designated as Schedule 40, Schedule 80, etc.

Set

The permanent deformation that remains in material after bending, measured as a residual angle or radius.

Springback

The elastic recovery of material after bending forces are removed, causing the material to partially return toward its original shape.

T

Tangent Length

The straight portion of material between the center point of one bend and the start of the next bend.

Tangent Point

The exact location where the straight section of material meets the curved section in a bend.

Tolerance

The allowable variation in dimensions or angles for a finished part, specified in the engineering drawing.

U

Ultimate Tensile Strength

The maximum stress a material can withstand before breaking or failing under tension.

W

Wall Thickness

The thickness of material in a pipe, tube, or hollow section, measured in inches or millimeters.

Weld Bending

Bending of welded pipe joints or seams, requiring special care to avoid cracking or stress concentration at weld lines.

Wrinkle

An undesirable fold or crease on the inside surface of a bend, typically caused by inadequate mandrel support or excessive bending force.

Y

Yield Strength

The stress at which material begins to deform permanently, an important property for determining bending difficulty.

Z

Zero-Based Dimensioning

A dimensioning system where all measurements reference a single origin point, improving accuracy in complex bent parts.
