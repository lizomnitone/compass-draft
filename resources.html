Frequently Asked Questions

▼

Do you do small jobs?

Yes. We are doing small jobs all the time, some of them for tiny parts and small quantities.

Do you only do work for businesses?

No. We work for individuals for personal projects as well as what we do for businesses.

Can I supply my own material?

Yes. We can supply material but will work with yours if that's your preference. If you like, we can quote material and labour separately so you can decide if you'd want us to supply material or not.

Do you only work with steel?

No. We work with almost all metals, including carbon steels, stainless steels, aluminum, copper, brass, spring steel, and many others.

Do you do any fabrication or only bending?

We do some fabrication, mostly in steel, but we can also weld aluminum. We also offer stud-welding for concrete anchors and some machining services in our in-house machine shop. These extra capabilities make it easier for our customers to do one-stop shopping.

Pipe Lengths

Type Description
SRL (Single Random Length) Seamless pipe approximately 20' long, varying from 17' to 24' (±) - hence "random" length
DRL (Double Random Length) Seamless pipe approximately 40' long (±), roughly double the length of SRL, varying from 37' to 44' (±)
TRL (Triple Random Length) Welded/ERW pipe approximately 60' random lengths, varying by manufacturer from 55' to 65'

Note: Seamless pipe is seldom available in any length other than singles (SRL) and doubles (DRL). CSA grades of welded pipe (ERW - electric resistance welded) are usually available only as triple random lengths (TRL).

Bend Radius Specifications

Note: D represents the nominal diameter. Radius is measured to the centre line of the pipe. For example: 4" diameter at 20D = 4" x 20 = 80" centerline radius = 2032 mm. Standard oil patch radius is typically 20D.

Radius Type Description & Example
15D Nominal diameter multiplied by 15 (e.g., 3" ø 15D = 3 x 15 = 45" radius = 1143 mm)
20D Nominal diameter multiplied by 20 (e.g., 4" ø 20D = 4 x 20 = 80" radius = 2032 mm) - standard oil industry radius
50D Nominal diameter multiplied by 50 (e.g., 4" ø 50D = 4 x 50 = 200" radius)
Elbows (3D, 5D, etc.) Long-radius and tight-radius elbows available in various standard configurations
Metric or Imperial Measurements Radius may also be specified directly in measurement (e.g., 5'-0" radius)

Pipe Schedule Designations

XH (Extra Heavy) / XXH (Double Extra Heavy): Also referred to as XS (Extra Strong) and XXS (Double Extra Strong). Thickness varies by pipe size according to standard tables. These designations are typically used only for standard pipe sizes (A53B, A106B, A333-6).

Glossary

▼

A

Angle Correction

Adjustment made to the initial bend angle to account for springback, ensuring the final bend meets specification.

Annealing

Heat treatment process that softens metal and relieves internal stress, making material more ductile and easier to bend.

ASME

American Society for Metallurgical Engineering - the governing body for pipe and fabrication standards.

ASTM

American Society for Testing and Materials - the governing body for pipe and fabrication standards.

B

Back-Bending

A secondary bending operation performed in the opposite direction to correct or refine a previous bend.

Bend Allowance

The amount of material consumed or stretched during the bending process, used in calculations for flat pattern layouts.

Bend Radius

The measurement of the curve created by a bend, typically measured from the center of the curve to the inside edge of the bent material.

Bend Test

Test of the weld seam of welded pipe, performed to verify weld integrity and material properties (destructive test).

BL (Bill of Lading)

Official shipping document that records the shipment details, contents, and delivery terms for transportation.

3D/5D/10D/15D/20D

Radius of a bend expressed in multiples of the pipe's nominal size (not outside diameter). For example: 4" ø 20D = 80" centerline radius.

C

C.S. (Carbon Steel)

Carbon steel or mild steel, a common material used in pipe bending applications.

Cat (Category)

Temperature service category designation for pipe material (e.g., Cat1 for above 0°C service, Cat2 for low temperature service at specified temperatures like -45°C, Cat3 for low temp with different requirements).

Centre to End (C/E, C-E)

Dimension measurement from the centre line of one line of the bend to the other end of the bend.

Centre to T.P.

Dimension from the centre line of one line of the bend to the tangent point of the other end of the bend.

Centerline Radius (CLR)

The radius measured from the center of the curve to the centerline of the material (for tubes/pipe). The primary specification for bend design.

Charpy

Charpy v-notch toughness test for low temperature service material - a destructive test used to verify material properties.

Chord

A straight line measurement across an arc of a curve.

CL (Centre Line)

The imaginary line through the centre of an object, used as a reference for measurements.

Coil Bending

A specialized bending technique that creates continuous spiral or helical curves for cooktop burners and custom equipment.

Cold Bending

Bending material at room temperature without heating, commonly used for most commercial bending applications.

CSA (Canadian Standards Association)

The standards organization that determines pipe, pipeline, and bending specifications, requirements, and tolerances for Canadian applications.

CSA Grade

Pipe grade classification for seamless or welded carbon steel pipe according to CSA standards.

D

D (Radius Multiplier)

The radius multiplier used in bend specifications where D x pipe diameter = centerline radius (e.g., 6" ø / 20D = 6" x 20 = 120" CLR).

Deformation

The change in shape or dimensions of material as a result of bending. Can be elastic (recovers) or plastic (permanent).

Developed Length (D.L.)

The arc length of the bent section only, used for material calculations and engineering specifications.

Destructive Test

Testing method that must destroy the sample material in order to evaluate its properties, such as bend tests, tensile tests, and Charpy tests.

Dual Grade

Material which is certified to meet different grades, codes, or standards simultaneously (e.g., A333-6/Gr359 would be certified to both ASTM A333-6 and CSA Gr359 standards).

Diameter (dia., D, Ø)

The measurement across the widest part of a circular or cylindrical object, represented by the symbol Ø.

Die

A shaped tool that forms the outer profile of the bend. Different dies produce different bend radii and shapes.

Draw Bending

A bending method using a mandrel (internal support) and die to create precise, tight-radius bends for pipe and tube work.

E

Elastic Recovery

The tendency of material to partially return to its original shape after bending stress is removed, also known as springback.

Elongation

The amount a material stretches during bending, expressed as a percentage of original length.

F

Fabrication

The process of building, assembling, or constructing metal parts. Includes cutting, bending, welding, and finishing operations.

Flatness Tolerance

The allowable deviation from a perfectly flat surface, specified in engineering drawings.

Flat Pattern

The unfolded, two-dimensional representation of a bent part showing where bends will occur.

G

Gauge (Wire)

A standardized measurement system for wire or sheet metal thickness. Lower gauge numbers indicate thicker material.

Grain Direction

The directional alignment of crystals in metal, which affects bending performance and material strength.

Gr (Grade)

Material type or grade classification for pipe and steel products, such as Gr359, Gr414, Gr448.

Grip

The straight section of material used by bending machinery to hold the ends of material during bending (can be the tangent section).

Hardness

The hardness of material as tested with various types of equipment and measured by various standards (e.g., Vickers, Brinnell hardness tests).

H

Heat Number

The manufacturer's identification number or batch number for a specific heat (batch) of material.

Heat Treating

Controlled heating and cooling of metal to modify its properties, hardness, and workability.

HIC (Hydrogen Induced Cracking)

Hydrogen induced cracking resistant material designed for very high sulfur content environments.

Heat Treatment / Stress Relief / PBHT (Post-Bend Heat Treatment)

Controlled heating and cooling process performed in a furnace to change a material's properties and relieve internal stresses after bending.

Hot Bending

Bending metal at elevated temperatures to reduce stress and allow for tighter bends in materials that would otherwise be difficult to bend cold.

I

ID (Inside Diameter)

The measurement of the diameter on the inside surface of a hollow pipe or tube.

Induction Bending

A bending method using electromagnetic induction to heat a localized area of the pipe while bending, reducing springback and enabling tighter radii.

Inside Radius

The measurement of the curve on the inner surface of a bend, from the center point of the curve.

IR (Inside Radius)

The radius measurement to the inside edge of a bent object.

L

LPI (Liquid Penetrant Inspection)

Non-destructive testing method using liquid dye and black light to detect surface and subsurface imperfections, used for similar purposes as MPI.

M

Mandrel

An internal support tool inserted into a tube or pipe during bending to prevent collapse, wrinkles, or deformation of the inside surface.

Manufacturer

The company or entity that produces the pipe or material.

Material Specification

The documented properties and grade of material, including composition, strength, and workability characteristics.

MPI (Magnetic Particle Inspection)

A non-destructive testing method using magnetic particles to detect surface and subsurface defects in ferromagnetic materials.

MTR (Mill Test Report)

Official documentation from the pipe manufacturer (also called mill certificate or mill cert.) that certifies material properties and composition.

Mtl (Material)

Abbreviation for material type or material specification.

Microhardness

A series of hardness tests performed on a cross-section cut from a pipe to test hardness throughout the pipe wall thickness.

Mill

The pipe manufacturer or pipe mill - the facility that produces the pipe.

Mill Cert / Mill Certificate

Official mill test certificate, same as MTR (mill test report).

N

Nominal

The name or designation given to a pipe size, which is not always a direct relation to its exact size (e.g., 6" nominal pipe is actually 6.625" in outside diameter).

Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)

Inspection methods that evaluate material properties without damaging the part, including ultrasonic, X-ray, and dye penetrant testing.

Nondestructive Test

Testing which does not destroy the sample material, allowing the part to remain usable after inspection.

Neutral Axis

The imaginary line through the thickness of material where there is neither compression nor elongation during bending.

O

OD (Outside Diameter)

The measurement of the diameter on the outside surface of a pipe or tube.

Offset Bending

Creating a parallel displacement in a pipe or tube by making two bends in opposite directions.

OR (Outside Radius)

The radius measurement to the outside edge of a bent object.

Outside Radius

The measurement of the curve on the outer surface of a bend, measured from the center point of the curve.

P

P.O. (Purchase Order)

Official order number used to order goods or services from a supplier.

Plastic Deformation

Permanent change in shape of material that does not recover after stress is removed, the desired result in bending operations.

Plus or Minus (±)

Symbol indicating approximate tolerance or variation range for a specification.

Pressure Testing

Hydraulic or pneumatic testing of bent pipes and tubes to verify structural integrity and pressure ratings.

Profile

The cross-sectional shape of material being bent, such as round tube, square tube, channel, angle, or flat bar.

R

Radius

The distance from the center of a curve to any point on the curve, expressed in inches or millimeters.

Rise

The measurement from the middle of chord length to the arc of a portion of a curve, used to verify bend geometry.

Roller

A rotating cylindrical tool used in roll bending to progressively deform material into a curved shape.

Roll Bending

A bending method using rotating rollers to create smooth, large-radius curves in various profiles and materials.

S

Schedule

A standardized wall thickness classification for pipes and tubes, designated as Schedule 40, Schedule 80, etc.

Set

The permanent deformation that remains in material after bending, measured as a residual angle or radius.

Springback

The elastic recovery of material after bending forces are removed, causing the material to partially return toward its original shape.

SS (Sour Service)

Service designation for pipe and materials used in sulfur gas areas or high-hydrogen sulfide environments.

Sweet

Designation for pipe that is not sour service (does not need sour service material specifications).

Shrink Sleeves

Polyurethane coating in sheet or tube form used to coat pipe by heat shrinking, applied for protection and insulation.

T

Tag

Project reference or marking information used to identify and track bent components.

Tangent

The straight section of pipe on each end of a bend, connecting the bend to the rest of the assembly.

Tangent Length

The straight portion of material between the center point of one bend and the start of the next bend.

Tangent Point (T.P.)

The exact point on a bend where the bend starts or stops - the transition point from straight to curved material.

Tape Wrap

Rubbery coating applied by wrapping continuously around pipe for protection and insulation purposes.

Tensile

Tensile strength of material - a destructive test that measures the maximum stress a material can withstand under tension.

Tolerance

The allowable variation in dimensions or angles for a finished part, specified in the engineering drawing.

Transition Piece

Short length of pipe machine thinner on one end to join two pipes of different wall thickness. Transitions can also be made on the ends of bends to eliminate the need for a separate transition piece.

U

Ultimate Tensile Strength

The maximum stress a material can withstand before breaking or failing under tension.

U.T. (Ultrasonic Testing)

Non-destructive testing method used to measure wall thickness and detect internal flaws in pipe and materials.

W

Wall

The wall thickness of hollow material (pipe, tubing), measured in inches or millimeters.

Wall Thickness

The thickness of material in a pipe, tube, or hollow section, measured in inches or millimeters.

Weld Bending

Bending of welded pipe joints or seams, requiring special care to avoid cracking or stress concentration at weld lines.

WO (Work Order)

Shop order with detailed information for processing and tracking jobs through the bending facility.

Wrinkle

An undesirable fold or crease on the inside surface of a bend, typically caused by inadequate mandrel support or excessive bending force.

Y

Yield

Yield strength of material - the stress at which material begins to deform permanently, an important property for determining bending difficulty.

Yield Strength

The stress at which material begins to deform permanently, an important property for determining bending difficulty.

YJ (Yellow Jacket)

Polyurethane coating (may be white or other colors) applied to pipe for protection and insulation. Available in different types: YJ1, YJ2, YJ2K - representing different coating process and material variations.

Z

Zero-Based Dimensioning

A dimensioning system where all measurements reference a single origin point, improving accuracy in complex bent parts.
