Civil Infrastructure

Metal bending solutions for bridges, water systems, and public infrastructure.

Overview

Compass Bending serves the civil infrastructure sector with precision metal bending for bridges, water and utilities systems, transportation infrastructure, and public works projects. Our expertise ensures components that meet stringent performance requirements and industry standards.

Applications

Civil infrastructure projects require precision bending for critical structural and functional components.

Application Typical Uses Bending Solutions
Bridges Curved girders, arches, handrails, structural supports Large-radius structural bending, steel and specialty materials, project coordination, complete documentation.
Water & Utilities Pipe bends for water distribution, sewer systems, treatment facilities Pressure-rated bends, corrosion-resistant materials, material traceability, hydrostatic testing.
Transportation Infrastructure Highway guardrails, bridge components, protective barriers Consistent quality, high-volume capability, coordination with project specifications.
Public Works Railings, barriers, access structures, site amenities Durability and safety focus, coordination with site requirements, long-term performance.

Quality & Standards

Infrastructure components must meet rigorous performance specifications and industry standards. We work closely with project teams to ensure all components meet specifications and provide complete documentation.

Testing & Inspection

Test Type Description
Mill Test Reports (MTR) Comprehensive material certification and traceability documentation for all pipe and structural stock. Certifies mill chemistry, strength properties, and heat numbers. Complete bend records issued for all code work.
Non-Destructive Examination (NDE) Ultrasonic inspection — detects internal flaws, laminations, and looks for non-homogenous material. Hardness testing — verifies material hardness properties per code requirements. X-ray inspection — confirms internal integrity and weld quality. Magnetic Particle Inspection (MPI) — reveals surface and near-surface defects as required. Dye Penetrant Inspection (DPI) — highlights surface imperfections and cracks as required.
Destructive Testing Metallurgical laboratory testing as required by code. Tensile testing — confirms material strength properties. Yield testing — verifies yield strength. Charpy impact testing — verifies low-temperature service capability and brittle fracture resistance. Reverse guided bend test — ensures weld integrity and penetration on welded material.
Dimensional Inspection Verification of bend radii, angles, center-to-end measurements, and overall dimensions to drawing specifications. All critical dimensions verified to tolerance.
Material Certifications Complete documentation retained indefinitely for traceability and regulatory compliance. Complete bend reports issued for all code work. Includes MTRs, bend reports, hardness data, and all supporting documentation.
