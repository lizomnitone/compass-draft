Bend Radii Specifications

Pipeline bends are engineered to meet design specifications and installation requirements across a wide range of applications.

Radius Type Bend Radii Range Common Applications

D = nominal pipe diameter. Example: For 4" pipe, 3D = 12" radius, 10D = 40" radius. All bends can be engineered to custom radii as required by project specifications.

Specialized Pipeline Applications

Lines Requiring Pulled Liners

For corrosive or high sulfur content environments, pipeline systems often use flexible plastic liners (HDP material) pulled through installed pipe. These applications require larger bend radii to minimize drag during liner installation. We specialize in bends engineered for liner pull-through, typically ranging from 3D up to 20D+ depending on liner specifications and field conditions.

Concentric Bends

Multi-line installations in the same trench require bends with different radii to maintain parallel spacing between pipes. Depending on pipe sizes and spacing requirements, these can range from 30D to 60D or custom specifications. We provide precise engineering and bending to accommodate complex trench layouts and multiple pipeline configurations.

Quality & Documentation

Every pipeline bend is subject to rigorous quality control and complete documentation to ensure compliance with oil and gas standards.

Testing & Inspection

Test Type Description
Mill Test Reports (MTRs) Comprehensive material certification and traceability for all pipe and tube stock. Complete bend record issued for all code work.
Non-Destructive Examination (NDE) Ultrasonic inspection — detects internal flaws and laminations. Hardness testing — verifies material properties. X-ray inspection — confirms internal integrity. Magnetic Particle Inspection (MPI) — reveals surface and near-surface defects. Dye Penetrant Inspection (DPI) — highlights surface imperfections.
Destructive Testing Tensile/yield testing — confirms material strength properties. Charpy impact testing — verifies low-temperature service capability (typically -8°C to -45°C per CSA codes). Reverse guided bend test — ensures weld integrity and penetration on ERW (electric resistance welded) pipe.
Pressure Testing Hydrostatic testing to verify bend integrity and confirm pressure ratings
Dimensional Inspection Verification of bend radii, angles, and overall dimensions to drawing specifications
Material Certifications Complete documentation retained indefinitely for traceability and regulatory compliance
